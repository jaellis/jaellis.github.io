Research


I completed my undergraduate work at Franklin and Marshall College, where I studied the Biological Foundations of Behavior.

After graduation, I received a Post Baccalaureate Intramural Research Training Award which allowed me to study visual processing and emotion in the Laboratory of Brain and Cognition at the National Institutes of Health (NIH) for the next two years.

I moved to Chicago in 2010 and accepted a position at the University of Illinois at Chicago (UIC) where I was an fMRI data analyst. In two years, my team published 6 peer-reviewed papers exploring the bio-markers of pediatric mood disorders like Bipolar Disorder.

I helped pioneer our use of Independent Component Analysis (ICA) in producing statistical inferences based on our subject population.

I joined the Google [x] User Research team at a pivotal point for Project Glass. The product was slated for shipment to developers in a matter of months and the user interface (UI) needed a fundamental redesign. I conducted benchmark studies of Project Glass, and a separate comparison of multiple Android UIs that led to a decision on the primary user interface and general design improvements. Independently, I also ran a 40-person, week-long benchmark of two Android OS versions to compare with task performance on Project Glass UIs.
